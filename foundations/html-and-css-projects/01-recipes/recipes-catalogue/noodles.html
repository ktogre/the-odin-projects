Stir-Fry Noodles

Photo by ikhsan baihaqi on Unsplash

Description

Stir-fried noodles is a quick and delicious dish packed with colorful vegetables and tender noodles tossed in a savory soy-based sauce.
It's simple, healthy, and perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 250g egg noodles (or any instant or spaghetti noodles)
  • 1 medium carrot (julienned)
  • ½ red bell pepper (sliced thinly)
  • ½ green bell pepper (sliced thinly)
  • ½ onion (sliced)
  • 1 cup cabbage (shredded)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional, for aroma)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic (minced)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the noodles: Boil noodles according to package instructions until just tender (not mushy). Drain and toss lightly in a teaspoon of oil to prevent sticking. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the vegetables: Wash and slice all vegetables thinly for even cooking.
  3. Heat the oil: In a wok or large frying pan, heat vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add minced garlic and sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Stir-fry the vegetables: Add onions, carrots, and bell peppers. Stir-fry for about 2 to 3 minutes until slightly tender but still crisp. Add cabbage last and stir briefly.
  5. Add the noodles: Toss in the cooked noodles. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil (if using). Mix everything thoroughly so the sauce coats the noodles evenly.
  6. Add seasoning and protein (optional): Season with salt and black pepper to taste. If using scrambled eggs, chicken, or shrimp, add them now and mix well.
  7. Serve hot: Garnish with a sprinkle of spring onions or sesame seeds if desired.
