Spaghetti Jollof

Photo by Krista Stucchio on Unsplash

Description

Spaghetti Jollof is a savory, tomato-based pasta dish.
It's often cooked with peppers, onions, and seasoning cubes, giving it that rich, smoky jollof-like taste.
It is often served with fried plantains, boiled eggs, or grilled chicken.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 3 medium tomatoes (blended)
  • 1 red bell pepper (tatashe)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (atarodo)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • ½ teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 1½ cups chicken stock or water

Steps

  1. Prepare the pepper mix: Blend tomatoes, red bell pepper, and scotch bonnets until smooth. Set aside.
  2. Boil the spaghetti: Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until almost done (al dente). Drain and set aside, don't rinse.
  3. Make the sauce: Heat oil in a pan, add chopped onions, and sauté until translucent. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 2 minutes to remove the raw taste.
  4. Add blended pepper mix: Pour in the blended mixture and cook on medium heat for 10 to 12 minutes, stirring occasionally until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Season the sauce: Add curry, thyme, seasoning cubes, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Combine spaghetti and sauce: Add the boiled spaghetti into the sauce. Pour in chicken stock or water as needed. Mix gently until everything is coated evenly.
  7. Add proteins (optional): Stir in sausages, shredded chicken, or beef. Reduce heat and allow to steam for 5 minutes.
  8. Serve hot: Garnish with sliced onions or vegetables. Best enjoyed with fried plantain or boiled egg.
